## TL;DR

- **Turnover is a $2.65M problem on a 1,000-unit portfolio** — $4,000/unit in make-ready costs plus $2,321 in vacancy loss per turn; at 42% annual turnover, it's the largest controllable NOI drain available to property managers
- **A 3% renewal rate improvement pays back Opiniion in under 2 months** — $190K in annual avoided cost vs. ~$30K platform spend; 6.3x ROI in the moderate scenario
- **Finance leaders are now the buying center** — PM company CFOs under NOI squeeze are approving or killing tech budgets based on avoided cost, not feature lists; Opiniion needs a model they can put in a board deck
- **The 5% gap is the wedge** — the industry targets 63% renewal but achieves 58%; Opiniion's pitch is that residents are leaving for reasons property managers don't see (maintenance gaps, poor onboarding, unresolved feedback) — exactly the signal Opiniion captures
- **Online reputation has a compounding financial effect** — properties with higher Google ratings fill vacancies faster; every 5 days shaved off the 40-day average vacancy cycle saves ~$290/unit/year across the remaining turnover base

## Context: Why This Model Exists Now

The Friday PropTech brief (April 17) identified that **finance leaders have become the new buying center at property management companies**. Three forces are converging:

1. **NOI squeeze**: Insurance up 55% since 2021 ($777/unit nationally; Houston >$1,200/unit). Maintenance materials 30% above pre-pandemic. 93% of PM companies saw expenses rise. CFOs are now scrutinizing every line of software spend.

2. **Retention gap**: Industry targets 63% renewal, averages 58%. 40% of at-risk renters say they would stay another year if their property invested more in maintenance responsiveness — the exact signal Opiniion captures but that PM operators are currently blind to.

3. **Accountable ROI era**: B2B SaaS is entering an "accountable ROI" era in 2026. CFOs want proof, not promises. Finance leaders now regularly participate in mid-market SaaS purchases. A vendor without a CFO-ready model loses deals at the redline stage.

Opiniion's backlog item: build a financial model that Opiniion's champions can bring to their finance leaders before the next sales cycle.

## The Opiniion ROI Model: Three Scenarios

### Scenario Framework

Opiniion captures resident feedback at five critical moments: tours, move-ins, maintenance requests, lease renewals, and move-outs. This creates a leading indicator of renewal intent that PM operators currently don't have, allowing intervention before a resident decides to leave.

**What academic and industry research supports:**
- "The link between satisfaction and renewals is undeniable" (Grace Hill / Kingsley Index — 7M+ residents surveyed annually)
- 40% of at-risk renters would stay another year with improved maintenance responsiveness (Buildium 2026)
- Managers systematically misattribute churn: they believe residents leave for "life changes," while residents cite poor maintenance, rent, and security — Opiniion's survey data corrects this attribution gap
- Properties with higher satisfaction scores experience lower vacancy loss and fewer turn costs (Grace Hill PerformanceHQ case evidence)

**Conservative assumption**: Opiniion helps improve renewal rate from 58% → 60% (+2pp). This is below what most retention-focused interventions achieve and represents a single year of modest improvement.

**Moderate assumption**: 58% → 61% (+3pp). Midpoint of expected impact based on industry benchmarks for satisfaction-driven retention programs.

**Optimistic assumption**: 58% → 63% (+5pp). The industry target. Achievable if Opiniion data is acted upon systematically.

### ROI by Scenario (1,000-Unit Portfolio)

|---|---|---|---|
| New renewal rate | 60% | 61% | 63% |

Even at 3x the cost ($90K/yr), payback is under 6 months at the moderate scenario. This is a CFO-defensible investment.

## Secondary Value Drivers (Compounding the ROI Case)

These effects are real but harder to directly attribute — include as "upside not modeled" in the CFO presentation.

### 1. Reduced Vacancy Days via Online Reputation
Opiniion's automated review generation helps communities build Google ratings. Higher-rated properties attract qualified leads faster, reducing vacancy duration.

- If average vacancy days drop from 40 → 35 (5-day reduction) for the 400 units that still turn:
- Additional savings: 400 × (5/30 × $1,741) = **$116,067/year**
- NAA data: concessions cost $82/unit/year and rose 30.9% YoY — communities with better organic reputation need fewer concessions

### 2. Staff Labor Savings
Each unit turnover requires significant site-team coordination: contractor scheduling, make-ready inspection, key handoffs, applicant screening. At 420 turns/year, even 2 hours of site-team labor per event at $25/hr = **$21,000/year** in avoided administrative cost.

### 3. Attribution Correction → Better Capital Allocation
Opiniion's data tells CFOs exactly why residents are leaving. If maintenance is the #1 driver and the property is already spending $500K on maintenance, redirecting even 5% of spend toward the specific issues driving churn could prevent more turnovers than any marketing campaign.

### 4. NOI Multiple Effect (Asset Valuation)
For institutionally-owned or investor-backed PM companies: a 1% reduction in vacancy and turnover can improve cap-rate-based asset valuation. At a 5% cap rate, $189K in annual NOI improvement adds **$3.78M** in asset value on a stabilized portfolio. This is a legitimate angle for REIT or institutional PM buyers.

## The Buyer Psychology: How PM CFOs Think

Research on PM company procurement dynamics (2025) reveals the following:

**What PM CFOs are looking for in SaaS proposals:**
1. **Avoided cost, not revenue upside** — PM CFOs are in cost-control mode; frame Opiniion as avoiding $190K in spend, not "growing revenue"
2. **Operational proof, not pilot promises** — Reference the Opiniion/Yardi case study (582 communities, 18-month dataset) as evidence that this works at scale, not just in a test
3. **Short payback period** — Under 12 months is "easy yes" for mid-market PM CFOs; under 6 months is "automatic approval"
4. **Concreteness over abstraction** — $189,630 per 1,000 units is more credible than "improve NOI"; use the model with their actual unit count
5. **Comparables that resonate** — Compare the Opiniion cost to what one extra turnover costs. "You pay for the whole year of Opiniion in 8 days of one vacant unit."

**The one-liner CFO pitch:**
> "We cost less than 8 vacant days. Every renewal we prevent costs nothing — and we're capturing the data that tells you why residents leave before they decide to go."

## The Buying Process: Where Finance Gets Involved

Per B2B buying research (2025), SaaS deals stall when:
- Finance leaders are brought in at the contract stage with no prior context
- The business case relies on industry averages rather than portfolio-specific data
- The champion can't defend the ROI to their CFO

**Recommendations for Opiniion's sales motion:**

| Stage | Tactic |
|---|---|
| Discovery | Ask: "What is your current renewal rate? What's your average make-ready cost?" — personalize the model |
| Proposal | Deliver a PDF with the buyer's own data plugged in; conservative/moderate/optimistic scenarios |
| CFO meeting | Lead with avoided cost, payback period, and the Yardi/Opiniion 582-community case study |
| Negotiation | Offer a 90-day renewal rate tracking commitment; create a built-in win/loss review |
| Expansion | Use portfolio-wide renewal rate data to upsell additional properties or analytics modules |

## Actionable Recommendations

### 1. Build the Interactive ROI Calculator
**What**: A simple web-based or Google Sheets tool where a PM company inputs unit count, current renewal rate, average rent, and average make-ready cost — and sees their personalized ROI projection.  
**Who**: Finance + Marketing + Product  
**Timeline**: 4-6 weeks to MVP  
**Expected Impact**: Shortens sales cycles; enables champions to build the CFO case themselves  
**Effort**: Medium  
**Evidence**: CFO-ready business case tools are the #1 deal-acceleration tactic in B2B SaaS (Gartner 2025); buyers who co-create the business case close 2x more often than those handed a generic deck

### 2. Commission a Formal Retention Impact Study
**What**: Partner with Opiniion's data team to produce a peer-reviewed analysis of renewal rate changes across the 582 Yardi communities (and any others) before vs. after Opiniion adoption. Similar to what Grace Hill's Kingsley Index publishes annually.  
**Who**: Finance / RevOps + Opiniion Data Science  
**Timeline**: Q2–Q3 2026  
**Expected Impact**: Creates a primary-source ROI claim that replaces industry averages in the sales motion  
**Effort**: High (but durable)  
**Evidence**: Grace Hill's Kingsley Index (7M+ residents/year) is their core go-to-market asset; Opiniion's equivalent could be a proprietary retention benchmark

### 3. Price Anchor Around Turnover Cost, Not Seat Count
**What**: Restructure Opiniion's pricing narrative (not necessarily the price itself) to be explicitly compared to turnover costs. "Our platform costs X per unit per year. One turnover costs $4,000. We pay for the whole portfolio in under N turnovers."  
**Who**: RevOps + Finance + Sales Leadership  
**Timeline**: 2-4 weeks to update sales decks and battlecards  
**Expected Impact**: Reframes price sensitivity; moves conversation from "is this worth the cost?" to "how many renewals do we need to break even?" (answer: 7-12 renewals on a 1,000-unit portfolio)  
**Effort**: Low  
**Evidence**: Value-based pricing anchoring is standard practice in vertical SaaS; connects directly to CFO's preferred metric (avoided cost)

### 4. Develop a "CFO One-Pager" Deliverable
**What**: A single-page PDF that Opiniion's champions can forward to their finance leaders. Contains: the model (conservative/moderate/optimistic), the key data sources (NAA, Buildium, Renew), the break-even calculation, and one customer quote.  
**Who**: Marketing + Finance  
**Timeline**: 2-3 weeks  
**Expected Impact**: Reduces deal stall at CFO stage; enables champions who can't articulate financials to do so credibly  
**Effort**: Low  
**Evidence**: Deals stall most often when late-stage stakeholders (CFO) see the contract before seeing a business case (Gartner B2B buying research 2025)

### 5. Create a "Portfolio Benchmark Report" Sales Tool
**What**: Using Opiniion's aggregated data, produce a quarterly benchmark showing average renewal rates, feedback response rates, and NOI impact across customer portfolios by market segment. Give buyers a "where do you rank?" scorecard.  
**Who**: Product + Finance + Data  
**Timeline**: Q3 2026  
**Expected Impact**: Creates an ongoing reason to engage; shows prospects the gap between their performance and Opiniion customer benchmarks  
**Effort**: High (ongoing)  
**Evidence**: NMHC, NAA, and Buildium all use annual benchmarks as top-of-funnel content; Opiniion could own the "resident experience NOI benchmark" category

## Open Questions

1. **What is Opiniion's actual pricing structure?** The model above uses $15-$50/unit/year. Knowing the actual ACV helps calibrate the ROI story for specific deal sizes.
2. **What renewal rate data does Opiniion have from existing customers?** Even a directional "our customers renew at X% vs. Y% industry average" claim, if real, is a primary-source fact that eliminates the need for industry proxies.
3. **How does the ROI model change for institutional vs. independent PM companies?** Institutional buyers care about asset valuation (cap rate impact); independents care about cash flow. Two versions may be needed.
4. **What is the competitive ROI claim from Grace Hill / Kingsley Index?** If Grace Hill is already making a quantified retention claim, Opiniion needs a comparably rigorous model.
5. **Is the CFO of the PM company or the property owner/investor the real financial decision-maker?** For third-party management companies, the PM company CFO manages tool spend, but the property owner cares about NOI. Understanding the distinction shapes which ROI story to tell.
