- **AppFolio earnings are in 48 hours** (April 23, 5 PM ET): Q4 2025 revenue hit $248M (+22% YoY), 2026 guidance is $1.1B–$1.12B. Realm-X adoption is accelerating (73% higher lead-to-showing conversion, 10 hours saved/week per user). Any resident experience or satisfaction module announcement is a code red for Opiniion.

- **Rent Manager is iterating fast in SMB**: Q1 2026 release recap includes Orion AI, market rent workflow, 10 new tech integrations. Best option for small and mid-sized operators who can't afford AppFolio or Yardi pricing.

### 2. Entrata Launches "Industry's First" Agentic Property Management — March 24, 2026

**What happened:** On March 24, 2026, Entrata announced a major evolution of its Operations Experience Platform (OXP), introducing more than 100 operational workflows that now execute as AI agents. This covers leasing, maintenance, accounting, payments, and resident operations — all from a single system.

**OXP Studio:** A new centralized workspace where operators can:
- Activate and manage AI agents across the portfolio
- Monitor agent performance in real-time
- Connect company-specific SOPs and training materials to agents
- Build custom workflows with Agent Builder (no-code)

**Context:** Entrata received a $200M minority investment from Blackstone in May 2025 at a $4.3B valuation. That capital is now deployed in product. The "Autonomous Property Management" branding is deliberate and aggressive.

**What's missing from Entrata's announcement:** Entrata's agentic platform works inside the Entrata ecosystem. Property managers not already on Entrata cannot access OXP Studio. The agents are optimized for leasing and operational workflows — not resident satisfaction, NPS benchmarking, or retention-focused feedback loops. This is the gap.

**Relevance to Opiniion:** Entrata is the most technically aggressive competitor, but they are building for Entrata-native customers. The 100+ agent claim covers routine operations (invoice routing, maintenance coordination, lease renewals) — not resident relationship intelligence. Opiniion's cross-platform position and resident satisfaction focus remain differentiated. If a PM company running Entrata wants to benchmark resident sentiment across properties, they still need Opiniion.

### 3. RealPage Post-DOJ: Fintech Rebuild, Buildium Gap Stays Open

**DOJ Settlement (November 2025):** RealPage settled with the Justice Department without financial penalties or admission of guilt. Key restrictions going forward:
- Cannot use competitors' nonpublic, competitively sensitive data in its pricing tools at runtime
- Cannot use active lease data to train pricing models (limited to data aged 12+ months)
- Court-appointed compliance monitor installed
- Revenue management software = ~7% of total RealPage revenue

**New CEO, New Direction:** Dirk Wakeham (former RealPage president 2007–2013) returned as CEO in November 2025, replacing Dana Jones. Wakeham has been conducting a customer listening tour; clients are asking about AI strategy and post-settlement direction.

**Fintech Pivot:** Chief Fintech Officer Zahir Khoja (ex-CEO of Wave Financial, formerly Afterpay and Mastercard) joined to lead payments, financial services, and resident-focused financial offerings. RealPage's AI is going deeper into screening, payment processing, and financial infrastructure — not resident experience.

**Buildium update:** RealPage acquired Buildium for $580M in 2019. Buildium serves 17,000+ customers with 2M+ units in SMB. RealPage's stated plan for Buildium includes adding "click-on capabilities" for leasing experience, payment processing, and utility fee recovery — but no resident satisfaction or NPS tooling.

**Relevance to Opiniion:** The Buildium channel opportunity is more confirmed than ever. RealPage's investment thesis for Buildium is financial services and leasing workflow, not resident relationship management. Buildium customers (small-to-midsize PM companies, 10K-2M units) have zero native resident satisfaction tooling and an ownership structure (RealPage) that has explicitly chosen not to build it. This is a channel sale waiting to happen.

### 4. Yardi Virtuoso — Deeper Than It Looks, But Laterally Focused

**Virtuoso platform (launched YASC September 2025):** Three pillars:
- **Virtuoso Support:** In-app AI assistant (Voyager 8 + RentCafe CRM IQ). Resolves 78% of support queries without escalation. Reduces onboarding time.
- **Virtuoso AI Agents:** Customizable agents for maintenance coordination, vendor invoice routing, and month-end reconciliation. AP processing time cut by 60%. Financial reporting reduced from 20+ hours to under 5 hours/property.
- **Virtuoso Connectors:** First-ever property management connector for Anthropic's Claude. Users can ask questions against live Yardi data ("Which properties are likely to exceed budget next quarter?").

**New vertical products (April 2026):**
- Yardi MH Manager: All-in-one for institutional manufactured housing (Voyager-powered)
- Self-storage Matrix reports: Elevated focus on storage sector performance
- AI for public housing authorities: Phased rollout throughout 2026

**What Yardi is NOT doing:** Building resident satisfaction or NPS benchmarking tools for conventional multifamily. Yardi's AI investments are concentrated in financial reconciliation, maintenance ops, and new verticals (manufactured housing, self-storage, public housing).

**Relevance to Opiniion:** Yardi's 78% support-query resolution and 60% AP time reduction create a compelling internal efficiency narrative — but Yardi customers still lack resident sentiment intelligence. The Yardi Marketplace partner listing backlog item (added April 13) remains valid. Getting listed as a Yardi Marketplace partner gives access to institutional Yardi customers who have a growing resident experience gap.

**Relevance to Opiniion:** Rent Manager is a fast-moving SMB platform without a resident satisfaction module. Its customer base (independent operators, mixed portfolios) overlaps with Opiniion's smaller customer segment. The 10 new integrations in Q1 alone suggest an open-API, partner-friendly posture. Worth a partnership conversation.

### 6. ResMan — Steady Platform, No Major AI Push

**Current state:** ResMan's AI footprint is limited to:
- Leasing Assistant: AI chatbot for marketing and lead follow-up
- Fraud detection module
- Marketing Suite with CRM and Contact Center

No announced 2026 AI agent initiative, no new funding, no major product launch since Q4 2025. ResMan remains focused on conventional multifamily and affordable housing (HUD, Rural Development, Tax Credit). BoardRoom dashboard provides real-time KPI visibility.

**Relevance to Opiniion:** ResMan is a stable but non-aggressive platform. Its customers skew affordable housing and mid-market conventional — a segment that is underserved by resident satisfaction tooling. ResMan's limited AI investment makes it unlikely to build native NPS or resident survey functionality in the near term.
