# PropTech & Property Management Industry Trends
**Date:** April 17, 2026  
**Topic:** Friday — PropTech & Industry Trends  
**Prepared for:** Head of Finance & RevOps, Opiniion ($20M ARR)

---

## TL;DR

- **AI has crossed the adoption threshold**: 58% of property management companies now use AI, up sharply YoY — the question has shifted from *whether* to *which platforms deliver real ROI*
- **NOI is under structural siege**: Insurance costs up 55% since 2021 (~$800/unit nationally), maintenance materials still 30% above pre-pandemic, expenses rising for 93% of companies — customers desperately need cost-reduction tools
- **Multifamily market normalizing, not crashing**: Vacancy at 4.4%, rent growth near flat (0.2% YoY), construction pipeline down 30% — fundamentals stabilizing, but Sunbelt operators under more pressure than coastal/Midwest
- **Retention is the new growth**: Industry targets 63% renewal rates but averages only 58%; 40% of at-risk renters would stay with better maintenance — exactly Opiniion's ICP pain point
- **PropTech M&A surging**: 163 deals in the first 11 months of 2025 vs. 134 in all of 2024 — consolidation accelerating, creating both risk (customers acquired by competitors) and opportunity (smaller tools being absorbed)

## Key Findings

### 1. AI Adoption Has Crossed the Tipping Point

**Finding:** Buildium's 2026 State of the Property Management Industry Report reveals 58% of PM companies now use some AI, with platforms like AppFolio Realm-X (saving users 10.3 hrs/week on task management, 11.9 hrs/week on communications) and Buildium's Lumina AI leading adoption. AppFolio is the only vendor rated for Autonomous Task Execution (80%) in G2's Fall 2025 report.

**Key data:**
- AppFolio Realm-X Leasing Performer automates lead response and showing scheduling 24/7
- Buildium Lumina AI focuses on human-oversight-first agentic tasks (tenant comms, maintenance coordination)
- AI fraud detection now exceeds 98% accuracy; 75% of PM companies report increased fraud attempts in 2026
- Most AI platforms show ROI within 90 days; full revenue optimization at 6–12 months

**Relevance to Opiniion:** The platforms your customers run on (AppFolio, Buildium, Yardi, Entrata, Rent Manager) are all deepening AI integration. Opiniion should be positioned as the *resident intelligence layer* that feeds these AI systems with richer signal — not a standalone tool. As competitors embed their own sentiment/feedback features, Opiniion's integration story becomes existentially important. The question: are Opiniion's integrations deep enough that switching costs outweigh building in-house?

---

### 2. NOI Squeeze Is Creating Urgency for Operational Tools

**Finding:** The cost structure of property management is deteriorating. Insurance costs have risen 55% since 2021 (from $502/unit in 2021 to $777/unit in 2024 nationally; Houston exceeds $1,200/unit). Maintenance materials remain 30% above pre-pandemic baselines with an additional 8% projected increase in 2026. Expenses have risen for 93% of PM companies over the past year.

**Key data:**
- CAM charges surged 12–18% nationally 2023–2026
- 94% of contractors report difficulty filling positions — skilled trades commanding premium wages
- 35–50% of excess maintenance spend is attributed to controllable operational factors (reactive dispatching, vendor fragmentation, duplicate work orders)
- Half of PM companies say technology adoption is their top strategy for cost-cutting

**Relevance to Opiniion:** When customers are under NOI pressure, the CFO scrutiny on software spend increases. Opiniion must have a clear cost-avoidance/NOI-impact narrative — ideally quantifying how improved resident satisfaction reduces costly turnover (turnover typically costs $3,000–$5,000/unit in make-ready + vacancy loss). If Opiniion can show "1% improvement in renewal rates = $X saved per 100 units," that's a compelling ROI story for a CFO audience. Finance leaders at PM companies are the new buying center.

---

### 3. Multifamily Market Fundamentals: Stabilizing, Not Thriving

**Finding:** The U.S. multifamily market is in a transitional phase. National asking rent growth averaged just 0.2% YoY as of January 2026. Overall vacancy is 4.4% — below the 2010–2019 average of 5.2% but under upward pressure in Sunbelt markets (which run ~200bps higher than coastal/Midwest). Construction deliveries fell ~30% YoY and are down 53% from their 2023 peak, setting up potential 2027–2028 tightening.

**Key data:**
| Market Type | Rent Growth (2026E) | Vacancy |
|---|---|---|
| Coastal/Gateway (NYC, SF, Chicago) | +2–4% | ~3.5% |
| Midwest (Cincinnati, Columbus) | +1–2% | ~4.0% |
| Sunbelt (Austin, Phoenix, Denver) | -1 to +1% | ~6.0%+ |
| National Average | +0.2% | 4.4% |

- 297,000 new units delivered in 2025 (down from 371,600 in 2024)
- Homeownership barrier: median home monthly payment is ~$1,200 more than avg apartment rent → sustained rental demand
- Renewal rates near 55% nationally — a tailwind for retention-focused tools

**Relevance to Opiniion:** Opiniion's customers in Sunbelt markets (Texas, Southeast) face the most pressure to retain residents and differentiate. These should be priority segments for upsell of advanced analytics features. Coastal/gateway customers may be easier to expand as their market strengthens. Understanding Opiniion's geographic revenue concentration matters for FY26 planning.

### 4. Retention Is the Industry's #1 Growth Lever — And Managers Are Getting It Wrong

**Finding:** The industry targeted a 63% resident retention rate in 2025 but averaged only 58%. Nearly 75% of managers expect retention to improve in 2026. However, a critical perception gap exists: managers believe residents leave due to life changes (moving, buying a home), while residents report they actually leave due to expensive rent, poor maintenance, and security concerns.

**Key data:**
- 40% of at-risk renters say they'd stay another year if their PM invested more in maintaining the property
- In 2026, renters are placing more weight on service and responsiveness when deciding to renew (Buildium)
- The cost to turn a unit vs. retain: turnover is significantly more expensive; retention ROI compounds
- PM companies with retention goals >70% has tripled since 2021

**Relevance to Opiniion:** This is Opiniion's core product-market fit. The platform captures exactly the signals (maintenance feedback, tour experience, move-in satisfaction) that predict churn before it happens. Opiniion's pitch to PM buyers should center on: "You think you know why residents leave — you're wrong. We give you the data to know for sure, and act before they go." The finance narrative: every 1% improvement in renewal rate across a 1,000-unit portfolio = $30K–$50K in avoided turnover costs.

---

### 5. PropTech Consolidation Is Accelerating — Opiniion Must Have a Clear Position

**Finding:** The PropTech M&A market surged to 163 deals in the first 11 months of 2025 — already ahead of the full-year 2024 record. Larger platforms (AppFolio, Yardi, MRI, Entrata) are acquiring point solutions and building or buying adjacent capabilities. The property management software market is valued at $3.61B in 2025, growing at 6.4% CAGR to $5.89B by 2033.

**Key data:**
- Vertical SaaS growing at 18–22% CAGR vs. 12–15% for horizontal SaaS
- Vertical SaaS delivers 3x higher retention than horizontal players
- Embedded fintech in PM software is growing — capturing interchange fees, reducing DSO
- Common switching triggers in 2026: unpredictable pricing growth, maintenance workflow gaps, inadequate reporting

**Relevance to Opiniion:** With 163 PropTech M&A deals in 2025, Opiniion's integration partners (Rent Manager, MRI Software, AppFolio) could become acquirers or become acquired. Opiniion should: (1) diversify its integration footprint, (2) deepen data-sharing so it's harder to rip out, (3) understand which platforms are likely to build competing feedback features natively. Finance should model "acqui-hire vs. standalone" scenarios as part of strategic planning.

---

### 6. Regulatory Complexity Is Growing — Creating SaaS Compliance Opportunities

**Finding:** The regulatory environment for rental housing continues to tighten. California enacted AB 628 (habitability standards), AB 414 (security deposit modernization), SB 610 (disaster relief requirements), and AB 325 (algorithm-based rent pricing restrictions effective 2026). Over 130 local rent control measures are under consideration nationally. Fee disclosure requirements are expected to impact operations and rent growth reporting.

**Key data:**
- California rent cap: 5% + CPI (max 10% statewide); Los Angeles RSO: 1–4%
- AB 325 restricts coordinated rent pricing algorithms (direct impact on RealPage-style revenue management tools)
- Disclosure requirements are expanding — transparency mandates increasing admin burden
- BTR (build-to-rent) is gaining momentum partly due to lower regulatory exposure than traditional multifamily

**Relevance to Opiniion:** Regulatory complexity increases PM companies' need for documented processes, audit trails, and compliance tools. Opiniion's resident feedback documentation could become a compliance asset — demonstrating responsiveness, fair treatment, and habitability standards via timestamped resident interactions. This is an underexplored angle for the platform.
