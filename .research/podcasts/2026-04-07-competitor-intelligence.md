## SEGMENT 1: ENTRATA GOES AGENTIC — AND THE THREAT IS REAL

**Alex:** Let's start with Entrata because it's the most urgent. Their OXP — Operations Experience Platform — just crossed a threshold. It's not just a PM software anymore. It's an orchestration layer that routes AI agents across your entire portfolio.

**Jordan:** Walk me through what that actually looks like.

**Alex:** So the announcement talks about 100+ workflows that now run as AI agents. Think: a leasing agent that schedules showings 24/7, a maintenance agent that routes work orders, an accounting agent that processes invoices. But then there's ELI Call Analysis — which listens to leasing and resident calls, generates scorecards, and surfaces performance trends. And then OXP Studio, which is their command center for all of it — you can monitor every agent, build custom ones, and connect your own SOPs directly to the workflows.

**Jordan:** And Entrata also has Colleen AI from their acquisition — which specifically focused on resident engagement, renewals, maintenance communication.

**Alex:** Exactly. So the resident experience layer — which is squarely what Opiniion does — is now something Entrata considers inside its platform scope. The question isn't whether Entrata customers will eventually have access to native resident satisfaction tools. They will. The question is whether Opiniion can articulate why *independent, cross-platform* resident data is worth more than whatever Entrata builds natively.

**Jordan:** And that answer exists. Opiniion measures across platforms — AppFolio customers, Yardi customers, Rent Manager customers. Entrata can only see Entrata properties. That benchmarking data is genuinely unique.

**Alex:** That's the narrative. Build it now, because Entrata is moving fast. For context on how well-funded this company is: Blackstone invested $200 million at a $4.3 billion valuation in May 2025. They are not experimenting. This is a conviction bet on owning the operating system of multifamily real estate.

---

## SEGMENT 2: APPFOLIO — THE NUMBERS BEHIND THE AI NARRATIVE

**Alex:** Now let's talk AppFolio, because there's a data story here that every property management SaaS vendor should be paying attention to.

**Jordan:** This is the February 2026 Benchmark Report?

**Alex:** Right. AppFolio surveyed its customer base — 21,403 customers managing 9.4 million units — and the headline finding is stark: property managers actively using AI expect 31% portfolio growth in 2026. Non-AI users? 12%. That's a 2.6x gap. Not directionally different — structurally different.

**Jordan:** And AppFolio is claiming 98% of their customers are actively using AI-native features.

**Alex:** Their AI product, Realm-X, has real metrics behind it. Lead-to-showing conversion is 73% higher when managers use Realm-X Flows. Units get filled 5.2 days faster. Renewal rates up 20%. NOI improvement of 2.8% on average. These aren't soft outcomes. These are things a property manager can take to their owner.

**Jordan:** So AppFolio is positioning AI as a performance management tool, not just efficiency.

**Alex:** That's exactly their framing. "From property management to performance management" — that's the tagline of their benchmark report. And financially, they're executing. FY2025 revenue was $951 million, up 20%. They're guiding to $1.1 billion in 2026. Q1 results come out April 23rd, and that's a critical date to watch.

**Jordan:** Why April 23rd specifically?

**Alex:** Because 45% of their customers say they're planning to consolidate their tech stacks toward unified platforms. That's the threat signal for any point solution. If AppFolio starts talking about resident experience, reputation management, or NPS-style measurement on that earnings call, that's an accelerant to the consolidation pressure.

**Jordan:** The counter-narrative for Opiniion has to be the independence angle — the fact that you're not AppFolio, which means your data isn't used to sell AppFolio services, and your benchmarks include every platform, not just one.

**Alex:** Correct. And that's a narrative that needs to be built *before* AppFolio tests it.

---

## SEGMENT 3: REALPAGE FALLS, AND THE RIPPLE EFFECTS MATTER

**Alex:** RealPage. Let's address the elephant in the room. The DOJ settlement is now finalized, published in the Federal Register in January 2026. The algorithmic pricing tool that made RealPage famous — and then infamous — is structurally constrained.

**Jordan:** What does "structurally constrained" actually mean?

**Alex:** The algorithm can no longer be trained on active lease data from competing properties. It's limited to historical data that's at least 12 months old. It cannot make pricing recommendations at a geography more specific than the state level. And RealPage cannot host meetings where competing property managers discuss pricing strategy using RealPage data. Simultaneously, they've settled $141.8 million in civil claims to renters.

**Jordan:** That's a business model disruption, not just a legal expense.

**Alex:** And it has a second-order effect on Buildium, which RealPage acquired in 2019. Buildium is now carrying the reputational shadow of its parent. Any property manager who's nervous about the RealPage scandal — and many are — is going to be cautious about Buildium even though it operates separately.

**Jordan:** For Opiniion, this is actually an opportunity. If you're selling to property managers who are coming off RealPage or Buildium platforms, the clean data governance story is a real differentiator.

**Alex:** And that's not hypothetical — regulators are now actively watching data-sharing practices in PropTech. Getting a clean one-page data governance statement in front of enterprise buyers is a low-effort, high-signal move.

**Alex:** Exactly the profile where Opiniion's product creates measurable ROI. This is a "make the call this week" situation, not a Q3 initiative.

## CLOSE: THE CONTRARIAN TAKE
