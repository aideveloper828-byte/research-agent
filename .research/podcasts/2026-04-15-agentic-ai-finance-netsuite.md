**[SEGMENT 1: WHAT "AGENTIC AI" ACTUALLY MEANS IN FINANCE]**

**JAMIE:** Let's start with the term. "Agentic AI" is getting thrown around a lot, but it has a specific meaning for finance teams. This isn't a chatbot that answers questions. Agentic AI is a system that can execute multi-step workflows autonomously — plan, act, evaluate, adjust — without human approval at every step.

**ALEX:** So instead of asking AI "what's the variance in marketing spend?" and reading the answer, an agentic system actually *runs* the close. It reconciles accounts. It identifies variances. It drafts the narrative. It routes exceptions to the right person. Then it waits for a human to approve the journal entry before it posts.

**JAMIE:** The human is still in the loop. But only at the decision points that matter — not at every data-gathering step.

**ALEX:** And the numbers on this are significant. A Forrester 2025 Finance Automation Report found that agentic AI handles exception scenarios 4.2 times more effectively than RPA — robotic process automation, the previous generation of close automation. And it requires 73% fewer annual rule updates, which means less maintenance overhead for your team.

**JAMIE:** For context: RPA breaks when the underlying process changes. An invoice format changes, a GL code moves, a vendor gets renamed — RPA falls over and someone has to fix the rules. Agentic systems adapt. That's the architectural difference.

**ALEX:** 67% of CFOs in a Q1 2026 CFO Dive survey said agentic AI workflow automation is their highest-priority finance technology investment this year. For the first time, it's not just "AI for finance" generically — it's specifically the autonomous workflow piece.

---

**[SEGMENT 2: NETSUITE 2026.1 — THE UPGRADE YOU ALREADY PAID FOR]**

**JAMIE:** Now let's talk about something very specific. If you're on NetSuite, this is actionable today. NetSuite's 2026.1 release rolled out starting in February and continues through April. And the AI features in this release are legitimately interesting.

**ALEX:** Three things to know. First: **Intelligent Close Manager**. It's a new dashboard portlet that gives your finance team a centralized, real-time view of period-end activity. It uses AI-driven exception detection to surface anomalies before they become close problems. And every flagged item is hyperlinked — click it, go straight to the issue, fix it.

**JAMIE:** Before this, the close was managed in spreadsheets or task lists. This is basically a control tower for your close that watches everything and tells you what needs attention. That's new.

**ALEX:** Second: **Payment Date Predictions**. NetSuite now uses machine learning to estimate when your invoices will be paid — not just the due date, but when customers actually pay based on historical behavior. This feeds directly into cash flow forecasting.

**JAMIE:** For a SaaS company at $20M ARR, cash visibility is critical. Knowing that a specific customer typically pays 12 days late, so model that into your three-month cash forecast — that's the kind of intelligence that used to require a separate FP&A tool.

**ALEX:** And third: **GenAI-enhanced bank matching and flux analysis**. Bank transaction matching now uses generative AI to extract richer structured data from bank feeds, which means higher auto-match rates and fewer manual reconciliations. And GenAI flux analysis automatically detects fluctuations above configurable thresholds and writes plain-language explanations for you to review.

**JAMIE:** That last one is significant for board prep. How much time does your controller spend writing variance narratives for the board deck? "Marketing spend increased $40K month-over-month due to the Q2 campaign launch." NetSuite can now draft that for you. Human reviews and edits — but the first draft is done.

**ALEX:** The kicker: all of this is already included in your NetSuite license. You don't need to buy anything. It's a configuration task. Ask your NetSuite admin to check if your account has been upgraded to 2026.1 and enable Intelligent Close Manager and Payment Date Predictions. That's it.

**[SEGMENT 3: THE TOOL LANDSCAPE — NUMERIC, FLOQAST, AND WHAT TO ACTUALLY EVALUATE]**

**JAMIE:** Now, let's talk about the independent tool market — because not everyone gets what they need from native NetSuite, and there are some significant funding rounds happening that signal where the market is headed.

**ALEX:** Numeric raised $51 million in a Series B led by IVP in November 2025 — $89 million total. Their pitch: take over close management, reconciliation automation, and cash matching on top of whatever ERP you're running. Their customer list includes OpenAI, Brex, Wealthfront, Plaid.

**JAMIE:** That's a very specific ICP: fast-growing, VC-backed, technically sophisticated companies with complex accounting. Multi-entity, revenue recognition complexity, audit prep pressure. If that sounds like where your company is headed, Numeric is worth a demo.

**ALEX:** Their cash matching rate is 90%-plus, and they have an AI rule builder that adapts to your specific reconciliation patterns. The comparison question you'd want to answer is: does NetSuite's Intelligent Close Manager get you 80% of the way there for free? Or do you need Numeric's additional capabilities?

**JAMIE:** And then FloQast — which launched something really interesting on March 31st. They call it the Visual Agent Builder. It's part of their Transform product, and it's essentially a drag-and-drop canvas for building close automation workflows. No code required.

**ALEX:** They call the building blocks "Skills" — pre-built accounting actions that snap together. Reconcile, flag, route, approve, post. You build the workflow visually, you can see it running, and when your process changes — new entity, different approval threshold — you update the agent yourself without IT.

**JAMIE:** The use case: a controller who wants to automate the intercompany reconciliation workflow can now build that agent without filing an IT ticket. That's a meaningful change in who owns close automation.

**ALEX:** If you're evaluating finance tooling, the decision tree looks something like this: start with NetSuite 2026.1 native features because they're free. If you have multi-entity complexity or need more sophisticated reconciliation automation, evaluate Numeric. If you're already on FloQast or want a dedicated close management platform with no-code agent building, look at FloQast Transform.

**[SEGMENT 4: WHAT GARTNER AND GRANT THORNTON SAY ABOUT AI ROI]**

**JAMIE:** Let's talk strategy for a second, because this is relevant if you're building a business case for AI tools internally.

**ALEX:** Gartner published a press release on March 24th — "CFOs Need to Rethink the ROI of AI Investments." The headline finding: CFOs are misjudging AI because they're applying a single financial return lens to all projects. And Gartner says that's the wrong framework.

**JAMIE:** Their prescription is a three-tier portfolio approach. Tier one: routine automation — AP processing, reconciliation, data prep. Measure cost reduction and hours saved. Tier two: analytical AI — forecasting, FP&A, anomaly detection. Measure forecast accuracy improvement and decision speed. Tier three: transformational AI — new business intelligence, competitive disruption. Measure strategic value.

**ALEX:** The key insight is that each tier creates value differently and on a different timeline. Your AP automation might pay back in 7 months. Your FP&A forecasting tool might improve decision quality for a year before you see it in revenue. If you evaluate both on the same ROI model, you'll chronically underinvest in the analytical layer.

**JAMIE:** And the CFO's role here is to be the person in the room who understands those distinctions and can make the case to the board.

**ALEX:** Meanwhile, Grant Thornton's Q1 2026 CFO survey of 230-plus finance leaders found that 68% plan to increase IT and digital transformation spending — the highest level in the 21 quarters they've been running this survey. That's five-plus years of data, and CFOs are at an all-time high on AI conviction.

**JAMIE:** 72% also expect net profit to grow over the next 12 months, which means this isn't defensive spending — this is offensive. Finance leaders are investing in AI because they see margin expansion on the other side.
